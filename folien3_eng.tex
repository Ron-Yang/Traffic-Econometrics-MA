\frame{\frametitle{The question about the age and income}
%##############################################################

Tricky questions!

\bi
\item Asking for the birth year is more neutral and gives the
  same information as asking for the age
\item Asking for age groups is less intrusive and often sufficient for
  the objective of the investigation \\[1em]
\pause \item Nobody wants to reveal his/her true income 
\item Nor do most people
  know it exactly. It also is conditional to after/before taxes,
  social security etc.\\[1em]
\pause \item Solution:
\bi
\item[(i)] Ask relative to a reference  (``the average income after
  taxes is 1\,300 \euro{} per month'')
\item[(ii)] Use categories (``much more, more, about the same, less,
  much less'') in a Likert scale
\ei
\ei
}

\frame{\frametitle{Choice-based conjoint analysis 2:
orthogonal design}
%###################################################
\begin{tabular}{|r|c|c|c|} \hline
$i$ & {\small Ped/bike travel time $T_1$}
  & {\small PT travel time $T_2$} & {\small PT ad-hoc costs $K_2$}
   \\ \hline
2 & \unit[10]{min} & \unit[10]{min} & \unit[1.50]{\euro{}}\\
18 & \unit[10]{min} & \unit[30]{min} & \unit[1.50]{\euro{}}\\
84 & \unit[30]{min} & \unit[10]{min} &\unit[3.00]{\euro{}}\\
99 & \unit[30]{min} & \unit[30]{min} &\unit[3.00]{\euro{}}\\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\vspace{1em}

\bi
\item No correlation (orthogonality) 
between the travel times of the two alternatives
\item Correlation allowed between attributes that are correlated in
  reality such as time and costs
  (here, $\text{Cov}(T_1,K_2)=0$ but $\text{Cov}(T_2,K_2)>0$)
\item Otherwise, dominant alternatives are nearly unavoidable in some
  choice sets
\ei

}
